# Why people like or dislike fingerprint blood-pressure apps

Research summary used to design PressWell (sources: Harvard Health, JAMA Internal Medicine / PMC, MedM, ASA rulings, App Store & Google Play reviews, 2024–2026).

## What people like

- **Convenience** — check something without a cuff nearby
- **Curiosity / peace of mind** — quick glance when anxious
- **Simplicity** — large buttons, few steps, no account
- **Privacy** — health numbers staying on the phone
- **History & doctor export** — trends and printable reports
- **Senior-friendly UI** — big type, high contrast, calm screens

## What people dislike (deficiencies we cover)

| Deficiency | What went wrong elsewhere | How PressWell responds |
|---|---|---|
| Misleading “medical” claims | Fingerprint/camera apps implied real BP measurement; ASA upheld complaints; Harvard warns they falsely reassure people with hypertension | Clear onboarding acceptance; every estimate labeled **wellness estimate, not a medical reading**; cuff logging is the recommended path |
| Dangerous inaccuracy | Popular apps misclassified many high readings as normal | AHA-style categories, crisis banner, “see a clinician” guidance; no claim of FDA clearance |
| Ads blocking the UI | Endless interstitial / redirect ads | **Zero ads** |
| Paywalls & surprise subscriptions | Charged before any useful feature | **Free core features**, no account required |
| Tiny text / hard for older adults | Interfaces built for young eyes | Extra-large type mode, high contrast, 44px+ tap targets |
| Broken “camera fingerprint” flows | Camera never opens; fake scan then ads | Reliable on-screen finger pad with timed guidance + demo mode |
| No real cuff logging | Apps that only “scan” leave people stuck | First-class **Log from cuff** with large steppers |
| Privacy & cloud lock-in | Forced sign-up | Data stored **locally** in the browser / device |
| Can’t share with doctor | Features gated behind paywall | One-tap printable history report |
| Cluttered dashboards | Stats, promos, schedules fighting for attention | One job per screen; home has two clear actions |

## Important scientific note

As of late 2024–2026, **no smartphone-only fingerprint or camera app is FDA-cleared** to measure blood pressure. Cuffless estimates from consumer phones are not accurate enough for diagnosis or medication decisions. PressWell treats finger checks as **wellness estimates** and encourages validated cuff monitors for care.
